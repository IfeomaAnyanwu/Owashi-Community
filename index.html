About us

Umuowashi Community

Owashi is an ancient community of Umuokoroukwu Ezuhu na Amadi Nguru in Nguru-Nweke Autonomous Community of Aboh Mbaise Local Government Area of Imo State. Owashi is made up of Umundalagu, Umugaa, and Umueziuka. The above is in order of seniority. Umugaa consists of Umuijeoma, Umuoparafor and Umuishi.
Our meetings are always on 'Awuruawu' basis and on an Orie-Ukwu market day, but ... issues that require urgent attention are tackled by the 'Nzes' that represent each Umunna. The following are the Nzes and Oke-Nzes who preside over our affairs as at this year 2023:
1. Nze I.G Nwaru
2. Nze F.Eke
3. Oke-Nze B.Inechi
4. Oke-Nze C. Njoku
5. Oke-Nze C. Anyanwu.

In a recently conducted census (2023), the following was the summary.
1. Number of Indigenes 4495
2. Number of resident Non Indigenes 436.
3. Number of Residential Houses 442
4. Number of Commercial buildings - 70.

This census was conducted so that the Owashi community can have a handy statistics on which the community development can hinge on. Owashi community is an urban community as it plays host to Aboh Mbaise Local Government Headquarters and its ancilliary departments, Aboh Mbaise General Hospital(a Secondary Healthcare facility), Aboh Mbaise Library Complex, Aboh Mbaise High Court Complex, Chief Judge's Residence and Mbaise Secondary School (Mbaise's premier institution).
